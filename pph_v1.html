What is PPH — recognise it now
⌄
DEFINITION — PPH IS PRESENT IF ANY ONE OF THESE
🔴 Blood loss ≥ 500 ml after vaginal delivery
🔴 Blood loss ≥ 1000 ml after C-section
🔴 Blood loss with signs of hypovolaemia — any amount
🔴 Fall in haematocrit ≥ 10% from admission
🔴 Requires blood transfusion
Primary PPH
Within 24h of delivery
Secondary PPH
>24h to 12 weeks after delivery

Tone (MC overall)
Atonic uterus — uterus not contracting
Trauma
Perineal/cervical tear · hematoma · rupture
Thrombin
Bleeding disorders · coagulopathy
Tissue (MC 2° PPH)
Retained placental tissue
ATONIC vs NORMAL UTERUS

Top: Contracted uterus — vessels compressed, bleeding stopped. Bottom: Atonic uterus — open vessels, blood pours out. This is the most common cause of PPH.

4
Atonic PPH confirmed
Uterotonic drugs — step ladder
⌄
Give all THREE simultaneously: Bimanual massage + Inj. Tranexamic Acid + Uterotonic drug
Step 1 — First line (WHO first choice)
Inj. Oxytocin
20–40 IU in 500 ml RL/NS IV infusion
IM: 10 IU if no IV access
Never IV bolus — causes hypotension, arrhythmia, cardiac arrest
Never in 5% dextrose — causes water intoxication
Step 2 — Add if oxytocin fails
Inj. Methylergometrine 0.2 mg IM
Repeat every 2–4h · Never IV (sudden BP spike)
CI: Hypertension · Pre-eclampsia · Heart disease · After delivery of first twin · Rh-ve pregnancy
Step 3 — Add simultaneously (add-on drug)
Tab. Misoprostol 800 mcg sublingual
WHO: 800 mcg SL · ACOG: 600–1000 mcg SL/oral/PR
CI: Scarred uterus (previous rupture) · Safe in asthma patients
Step 4 — Gold standard if above fail
Inj. Carboprost (PGF₂α) 250 mcg IM
Every 15–90 min · Maximum 8 doses (2 mg total)
CI: Asthma · Pulmonary hypertension · Suspected amniotic fluid embolism
Tranexamic acid — give with all above
Inj. Tranexamic Acid 1g IV
In 100 ml NS over 20 min · Give within 3h of onset of PPH
Repeat 1g if bleeding persists ≥ 30 min OR reappears within 24h
Fast IV rate causes hypotension
SYNTOMETRINE (if available)
Fixed combination: 5 IU Oxytocin + 0.5 mg Methylergometrine · Expensive · Not first line
5
If unresponsive to drugs >30 min
Balloon tamponade — resource cascade
⌄
Continue uterotonics while inserting balloon. Principle: warm NS fills cavity → pressure on venous sinuses → bleeding stops.
Option A — Bakri balloon (if available)
Bakri Balloon Catheter
Fill with warm NS up to 500 ml (max capacity)
Remove after 12–24h if bleeding controlled
BAKRI BALLOON SYSTEM

Bakri balloon fills uterine cavity with NS — mechanical pressure stops venous sinus bleeding. Uterine balloon inflated inside, vaginal balloon controls cervical oozing.

Option B — Condom catheter (PHC/CHC — ₹20, always available)
CG Balloon — Foley No. 20 + condom
Same principle as Bakri · 96% success rate in Indian studies
HOW TO MAKE CONDOM CATHETER

(a) Cut 2 rings from Foley drainage tube (b) Inflate Foley bulb with 2–5 ml air, excise (c) Roll condom over catheter (d) Tie condom twice with rings, 1.5–2 cm on each end (e) Excise tip of Foley with blind end of condom 0.5 cm from tied ring

Insert assembled device into uterus
Fill with NS using 50 ml syringe — stop at 300 ml or when bleeding stops
Maximum 500 ml · Clamp catheter · Pack vagina loosely with gauze
Leave indwelling Foley catheter in bladder while condom catheter is in place
Remove after 12–24h if bleeding controlled
Option C — Nothing available → manual methods
Bimanual compression — sustained, continuous, by doctor until transfer
Aortic compression — fist on aorta at umbilicus level · check absent femoral pulse to confirm
MAST suit (Military Anti-Shock Trousers) if available
TRANSFER NOW — do not delay for surgical option
6
Surgical — district/tertiary
Surgical management — step ladder
⌄
Step 1 — Compression sutures
B-Lynch suture (most common)
Brace suture compresses uterus front to back · Uterus-sparing · Fertility preserved
B-LYNCH COMPRESSION SUTURE

B-Lynch suture — compression brace squeezes uterus. Also: Hayman · Cho square · Perriera · Gunshella sutures.

Step 2 — Devascularisation (if compression fails)
U/L uterine artery ligation (O'Leary stitch) → B/L uterine artery ligation
B/L anastomotic branch of ovarian artery ligation
Uterine artery embolization (UAE) if radiology available + vitals stable
O'LEARY STITCH — UTERINE ARTERY LIGATION

O'Leary stitch: B/L uterine artery ligation — 75% success rate. Needle enters myometrium 2–3 cm medial to uterine artery, exits lateral in avascular space of broad ligament.

Step 3 — Last resort
Internal iliac artery ligation → Subtotal hysterectomy → Total hysterectomy
Pelvic pressure packs: Umbrella pack or Parachute pack
Subtotal hysterectomy (cervix retained) is preferred — faster and less blood loss than total hysterectomy.
7
After 24h to 12 weeks
Secondary PPH
⌄
Common causes
Most common
Retained placental tissue (Tissue)
Infection
Endometritis · subinvolution of placental site
Management
Resuscitate as Step 2
USG uterus — look for retained products of conception (RPOC)
RPOC confirmed → Surgical Evacuation of Retained Products (ERPC)
⚠ Highest risk of Asherman syndrome in postpartum period
Antibiotics: Inj. Amoxyclav 1.2 g IV 8-hrly + Metronidazole 500 mg IV 8-hrly
Inj. Oxytocin 20 IU in 500 ml RL over 4h to promote uterine involution
⚠ ASHERMAN SYNDROME WARNING
D&C in postpartum period = highest risk for intrauterine adhesions (Asherman syndrome). Use suction evacuation or hysteroscopic removal wherever possible.

Postpartum Haemorrhage
Blood loss ≥ 500 ml after vaginal delivery (or ≥ 1000 ml after C-section) — the most common cause of maternal mortality in India.

Savitaben. 28 years old. 4th delivery. Village PHC. 2 AM.

Delivered normally. Placenta delivered. Suddenly — continuous bright red bleed. Soaks 3 pads in 10 minutes. Uterus feels like a soft bag of water. No tone. Relatives shouting.

Every concept below explains what is happening inside Savitaben right now — why the uterus is not contracting, which drug to give first, and why the condom catheter you make in 5 minutes at PHC can save her life when Bakri balloon is 90 km away.
