20WBCT — how to do it
⌄
2 ml venous blood → clean dry GLASS vial only
Never plastic — gives false result
Leave undisturbed at room temperature · Set timer 20 min
Tilt vial gently at 20 min — read result
CLOTTED
Normal
Repeat 30 min × 3h
NOT CLOTTED
VICC (blood lost all clotting power) → ASV now
Recheck at 6h post-ASV
Write "Clotted" or "Not Clotted" with exact time. Never "positive/negative".
4
Most important decision
When to give ASV
⌄
GIVE ASV IMMEDIATELY IF ANY ONE OF THESE
🔴 20WBCT not clotted
🔴 Spontaneous bleeding — gums, nose, IV site, urine
🔴 Ptosis — even partial drooping
🔴 Diplopia / ophthalmoplegia
🔴 Bulbar weakness — voice, swallowing, neck
🔴 Respiratory weakness or distress
🔴 Progressive swelling crossing a joint
🔴 Shock / hypotension
🔴 Dark urine with envenomation signs
⚠ Do not wait for lab results if clinical signs are clear. Go to Step 5 now.
5
ASV confirmed needed
Give ASV — exact steps
⌄
Pre-medication
Inj. Adrenaline 0.25 mg IM (1:1000) immediately before starting ASV
Child: 0.005 mg/kg IM · Draw 0.5 mg IM adrenaline in syringe — keep at bedside
Prepare ASV
Reconstitute each vial separately in 10 ml distilled water — swirl, do NOT shake
Transfer each reconstituted vial into 500 ml NS pint one by one
Total: 10 vials in 500 ml NS pint
Infuse
Run over 1 hour — approx. 8 ml/min · 60 drops/min macrodrip
Child: same 10 vials, same 500 ml NS pint, same rate · Adult = Child dose identical
Physician stays bedside entire infusion. Observe every 5 min for reaction.
Repeat dose — when to give
Neurotoxic worsening at 1h → repeat 10 vials, same method
20WBCT still not clotted at 6h after first dose → repeat 10 vials, same method
ASV DOES NOT WORK FOR
Hump-Nosed Viper (Hypnale hypnale) → supportive care only

Inj. Atropine 0.6 mg IV slow push first
Child: 0.02 mg/kg IV
Then Inj. Neostigmine 2.5 mg IV slow push

7
Haematotoxic track
Bleeding · DIC · AKI
⌄
Bleeding / DIC
Give ASV first — never transfuse before adequate ASV
Major bleeding persists after adequate ASV → FFP 10–15 ml/kg IV
No FFP → fresh whole blood transfusion
Strict bed rest · No NSAIDs · No aspirin · No IM injections in active DIC
AKI — monitor urine output hourly
Target urine output ≥ 0.5 ml/kg/hr
Oliguria → Inj. NS 200 ml IV bolus over 15 min → reassess
K⁺ > 6.5 · Urea > 300 · Anuria → urgent nephrology referral for dialysis
Compartment syndrome
Do NOT perform fasciotomy without specialist evaluation
Tense swelling + loss of distal pulse + paraesthesia → refer urgently
7A
Pregnancy track
Snakebite in pregnancy
⌄
ASV IS NOT CONTRAINDICATED IN PREGNANCY
Give full ASV dose immediately. Never reduce. Never delay for obstetric opinion. Untreated envenomation kills both mother and fetus.
Maternal monitoring
BP · Pulse · RR · SpO₂ — every 15 min
Urine output hourly · IV access both arms if possible
Fetal monitoring (if viable — > 20 weeks)
Fetal heart rate every 30 min
Ask about fetal movements at each assessment
Obstetric emergencies — watch for
Vaginal bleeding · abdominal pain · uterine contractions · absent fetal movements → urgent obstetric consultation
Neuroparalytic bite → intubate early — maternal hypoxia rapidly causes fetal hypoxia
Haematotoxic bite → coagulopathy causes placental abruption — correct with ASV first
ASV reaction in pregnancy
Inj. Adrenaline IM is first-line even in pregnancy — do not withhold
Untreated anaphylaxis is more dangerous to fetus than adrenaline
❌ Do not induce labour or perform operative delivery until maternal stabilisation and coagulopathy corrected — unless obstetric emergency.

Snake Bite
64,000 deaths annually in India. The bite that arrives quietly, kills overnight, and responds completely to a drug that costs ₹200 per vial — if you give it in time.
REAL PATIENT
Bhikhabhai. 52 years old. Farmer. Found by his wife at 5 AM.

He had gone to irrigate his field at midnight. Came home, said something bit his foot, went to sleep. At 5 AM his wife couldn't wake him. No swelling. No bite mark visible. No pain. By the time she brought him to the PHC — he was apnoeic. Common krait. Bit him at midnight. Killed him silently while he slept.
