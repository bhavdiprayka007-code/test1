EMERGENCY PROTOCOL
Eclampsia

Severe pre-eclampsia + generalised tonic-clonic seizures. Most common: antepartum. Most dangerous.

Step 1 — First 60 seconds
1
Turn patient left lateral
Prevents aortocaval compression + aspiration
2
Raise bed rails
Prevent fall injury during convulsion
3
Clear airway — suction if needed; jaw thrust if obstructed
Do not force mouth open
4
O₂ by mask — 8–10 L/min
Target SpO₂ ≥ 95%
5
IV access ×2 (18G or larger)
Draw blood simultaneously: LFT · S.Creatinine · CBC · coagulation

Do NOT leave the patient. Shout for help while doing above steps.

Step 2 — MgSO₄ (stop seizures + prevent recurrence)

NMDA receptor block → stabilises membrane potential → stops seizure propagation

PRITCHARD REGIMEN (India standard) — loading dose
IV route
4 ampoules of 50% MgSO₄ + 12 ml NS in 20 ml syringe = 4 g at ~20%
Inject over 15–20 minutes — never fast push
No pump: ~1 ml/min by hand
Never give 50% undiluted IV
IM route (simultaneously)
5 g (5 ml of 50%) in each buttock = 10 g total
Add 1 ml of 2% lignocaine each side — reduces pain
Deep IM with Z-track technique
MAINTENANCE DOSE — check all 3 before every dose
Knee jerk (DTR)
Must be present (+)
Respiratory rate
≥ 12 / min
Urine output
≥ 100 ml in last 4 hours

5 g IM q4h in alternate buttocks. Continue 24h post-delivery or 24h post-last seizure — whichever is later.

MgSO₄ TOXICITY
First sign: Loss of knee jerk — serum Mg ~9–10 mEq/L. STOP next maintenance dose immediately.
9–10 mEq/L
Loss of DTR
12 mEq/L
Resp. arrest
15–24 mEq/L
Cardiac arrest
Antidote — draw and keep at bedside before starting MgSO₄
Calcium gluconate 10 ml of 10% IV — slow push over 3 min

Oliguria alone is NOT a sign of toxicity.

Step 3 — Anti-HTN (prevent intracranial haemorrhage)

Indication: BP ≥ 160/110 mmHg. Target: systolic 130–140, diastolic 80–90. Do NOT overcorrect — hypotension → fetal distress.

1st line: Labetalol IV
Alpha + beta blockade → rapid BP reduction without reflex tachycardia
20 mg IV → check BP at 15 min → 40 mg → 80 mg → 80 mg
Max cumulative dose: 220 mg
Contraindicated: asthma, bradycardia
2nd line: Hydralazine IV
Direct arteriolar vasodilator
5 mg IV over 20 min → repeat 10 mg if needed
Max: 30 mg total. Watch for reflex tachycardia.
3rd line: Oral Nifedipine (NOT sublingual)
Calcium channel blocker — peripheral vasodilation
10 mg oral; repeat after 30 min if needed
Caution: combined with MgSO₄ → neuromuscular blockade
Step 4 — Definitive management
1
Terminate pregnancy — irrespective of gestational age
2
Mode: vaginal delivery preferred
3
Caesarean only for obstetric indication
4
Anaesthesia: general anaesthesia (not neuraxial — coagulopathy risk)

Refer to tertiary care immediately after loading dose + first anti-HTN

Recurrent seizure after MgSO₄?
1
Check: was maintenance given correctly? DTR present?
2
Additional 2 g MgSO₄ IV
2 amp 50% + 6 ml NS → 10 ml, inject over 10 min
3
Still seizing: Diazepam 10 mg IV slow push
Rescue only — causes neonatal respiratory depression
4
Reassess airway — intubate if GCS falling
NO DRUG? USE NEXT AVAILABLE

No labetalol → Hydralazine 5 mg IV

No hydralazine → Nifedipine 10 mg oral

No pump → 1 ml/min IV push by hand

Eclampsia with fetal distress
1
Check fetal heart sounds after stabilising mother
2
FHS abnormal → rule out abruptio placentae
3
Abruption confirmed or FHS not recovering → emergency caesarean
4
FHS normalises → continue eclampsia management + plan delivery
